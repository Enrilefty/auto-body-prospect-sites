How scheduling works

No mystery drop-off. No surprise repair path.

Mobile repair works best when the first conversation is specific. The request flow asks for the information a mechanic actually needs before committing your time.

01

Send the details

Vehicle year, make, model, location, symptoms, warning lights, and urgency.

02

Confirm mobile fit

Onsite Auto confirms whether diagnostics, maintenance, or repair can be performed safely at your location.

03

Authorize the work

You review the recommendation and approve repairs before additional work begins.

Good mobile fit

Problems that can often start at your location.

  • Check-engine lights and drivability symptoms.
  • Battery, no-start, belts, hoses, and basic electrical concerns.
  • Brake noise, pad/rotor concerns, and safety checks.
  • Oil changes, fluids, maintenance, and pre-purchase inspections.

Clear expectations

Some jobs still need a shop or lift.

Mobile service should save time, not create risk. If the repair needs shop equipment, unsafe conditions, or a deeper teardown, Onsite Auto can tell you before you commit to the wrong path.
